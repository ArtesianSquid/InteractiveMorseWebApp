Morse Code



Basic Information

Morse code is comprised of two signal units. “Dit”, which is represented by the character ·, and “dah”, which is represented by the character –

The dit (which is pronounced with a silent “t”) has a short single syllable sound whereas the dah lasts as long as three dits.

Dit: Dah:

There should be one dit’s worth of time between the dits and dahs of a single letter, one dah’s worth of time between letters in a word, and seven dit’s worth of time between words in a sentence.

"A": "CAT": "OK BYE":

History

Morse code was developed in the 1830s-1840s by Samuel Morse and a few other inventors. By transmitting electrical signals over a wire laid between stations, it was able to allow for complex messages to be sent across telegraph lines.

In 1844 the first message was sent from Washington D.C. to Baltimore Maryland and by 1866 a telegraph line had been laid across the Atlantic Ocean from the U.S. to Europe!
